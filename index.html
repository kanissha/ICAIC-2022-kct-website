Poster Presentation

Poster sessions are a valuable method for authors to present summary findings and engage with attendees for in-depth technical discussions. It is important that you display your results clearly to attract people who have an interest in your work. Poster presenters are responsible for the creation of their poster. Only listed authors may be presenters.

Abstract Preparation Guidelines:

An Abstract of between 300-400 words (plus between 2-5 references to support the evidence -base of the abstract content). (Any abstract that exceeds the required word count will not enter the review process). Abstract is required to include details of either research, development and/or innovation, with sufficient information to enable the review panel to decide on appropriateness for poster presentation in theme and context of conference. (Any research reported MUST have commenced at the time of the abstract submission). Abstracts should state briefly and clearly the purpose, methods, results, and conclusions/discussions of the work.

Poster Content:

Make sure that the specific sections (such as the background, methods, results, and conclusions) are easy to locate in the poster.

Illustrations:

They are the most important part of the poster and will attract the most attention. Colourful charts and graphs are the easiest to read. Explanatory illustrations should be used instead of text whenever possible. Use only static images and do not embed any videos or animation in your poster. Try to utilize the space on your poster as creatively as you can to attract the audience and make sure that the images are easy to understand.

Submission Requirements:

Offline:

The poster format must follow the following guidelines:
Poster size is selected by you. The size should be appropriate to convey your work. Common sizes include A0, A1, 2 x 3 feet, and 3 x 4 feet. Your poster should cover the key points of your work. Provide a brief overview of your work The title of your poster should appear at the top in CAPITAL letters, should be same as abstract title. The author(s) name(s) and affiliation(s) are put below the title. Make your poster as self-explanatory as possible. Place your POSTER ID on the top right corner of the poster. Use color to highlight and make your poster more attractive. Use diagrams, figures, tables, pictures, etc., rather than only text wherever possible. To maintain poster quality, posters printed on paper should be rolled and not folded for transportation.

Virtual:

Poster must be submitted in PDF format. It is recommended to make your poster in PowerPoint (Landscape or Portrait) and then save it as a PDF and upload in XXXX. Make sure that you do not exceed the limit of 5 PPT slides for your E-Poster. The top of the Poster should display, in the lettering of 18 Bold (Times New Roman), the following information: Title of your Poster, Name(s) of the Author(s) and Affiliation(s). Place your POSTER ID on the top right corner of the poster. Kindly use both UPPER and lower case letters for general content, as all-capital text is difficult to read. It is recommended to use 14 Times New Roman font style for the content of the poster. Total presentation time allotted for each poster shall be 7 minutes followed by Q&A session.

Paper submission

Policy on Plagiarism:

All papers submitted to ICAIC’22 will be checked for plagiarism (including self-plagiarism). If a paper is found to fall in the category of plagiarism, the paper will not be reviewed and automatically rejected. Even if a paper is accepted and paid for registration, the program committee has a right to reject the paper and will not publish the paper in the proceedings.

Paper Format:

  • Number of Pages: Papers should not exceed eight pages, including figures, tables in a paper
  • Page Size: A4 Size
  • File Format: Word and PDF (Single column)
  • Abstract: It is important that the abstract be a meaningful description of your paper
  • Keywords: Choose from three to eight keywords
  • Color: Use of color is encouraged, but since readers will usually print the papers in black and white, it is the author’s responsibility to ensure that all figures/plots be printed and understood in black and white

Paper Publication Fee:

Only accepted and presented papers at the ICAIC'22 conference are subject to a publication fee. Prior to the work being published in the Scopus-indexed journal, authors will be asked to pay a publishing fee. The cost will be determined by the type of journal. The payment method will be sent to the corresponding author via email. The charge must be paid by the corresponding author.
